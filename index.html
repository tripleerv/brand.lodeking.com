Typography

Primary headings use Knockout 50 Welterweight.

Knockout Welterweight is used for all main headings. It has a industrial and rugged look while maintaining legibility.

It should be used sparingly, and almost always in all caps.

Secondary headings use Knockout 48 Featherweight.

Knockout Featherweight is used for non-primary headings. It should also be used primarily in all caps.

Tertiary headings use Proxima Nova.

Proxima Nova was chosen for its clarity as well as its parallels with the parent brand, Triple E Canada.

Proxima Nova will be also used anywhere that is not a heading. This could include body copy, fine print, or some sub-headings.

For sub-heading use, Proxima should be set in BOLD ALL CAPS.

Product Naming Conventions

There are many different names used in the industry to classify our products. Please follow these guidelines to keep our naming scheme consistent.

Super-B

Refers to a b-train trailer. Capitalise the S and the B. Always use a hypen.

Tandem

Refers to a trailer with two axles.

Tri-Axle

Capitalise the T and the A. Always use a hypen.

Commodity

Use this in reference to a grain trailer.

Flat Deck

This refers to any trailer with a flat, rigid top.

Drop Deck

Refers to a flat deck with two different deck heights.
